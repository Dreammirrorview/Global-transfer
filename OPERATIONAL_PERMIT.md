## 1. LICENSE DETAILS

### 1.1 License Type
- **Category:** International Money Transfer Operator (IMTO)
- **Scope:** Inward and Outward International Money Transfers
- **Validity:** 5 Years (Renewable)
- **Expiration Date:** January 14, 2029

### 1.2 Authorized Operations
The Licensee is authorized to:
1. Receive inward international money transfers from abroad
2. Process outward international money transfers from Nigeria
3. Operate multi-currency accounts for customers
4. Provide foreign exchange services at approved rates
5. Issue and manage debit cards for international transactions
6. Maintain correspondent banking relationships
7. Offer digital banking services through web and mobile platforms

### 1.3 Geographic Coverage
- **Primary Market:** Nigeria
- **Correspondent Countries:** United States, United Kingdom, European Union, Canada, Australia, Japan, China, India, United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, Kenya, Ghana, and 138 additional countries

## 2. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### 2.1 Capital Requirements
- **Minimum Paid-up Capital:** ₦2,000,000,000.00 (Two Billion Naira)
- **Compliance Status:** ✓ VERIFIED
- **Certificate of Capital Importation:** CCI/2024/GBN/001

### 2.2 KYC/AML Requirements
The Licensee shall:
- Maintain comprehensive Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures
- Implement Anti-Money Laundering (AML) protocols
- Conduct enhanced due diligence for high-risk transactions
- Report suspicious transactions to the Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU)
- Maintain transaction records for minimum 7 years

### 2.3 Financial Reporting
- **Monthly Returns:** Transaction volumes and values
- **Quarterly Reports:** Financial statements and compliance status
- **Annual Audits:** Certified by CBN-approved external auditors
- **Special Reports:** As requested by CBN

### 2.4 Security Standards
- **ISO 27001:** Information Security Management
- **PCI DSS:** Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard
- **CBN Cybersecurity Framework:** Compliance required
- **Data Protection:** Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) compliance

## 3. OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Technology Infrastructure
- **Core Banking System:** CBN-compliant banking software
- **Disaster Recovery:** Backup systems with 99.9% uptime guarantee
- **Fraud Detection:** Real-time transaction monitoring
- **Encryption:** End-to-end encryption for all transactions

### 3.2 Human Resources
- **Compliance Officer:** CBN-certified
- **AML Officer:** Qualified and experienced
- **Internal Auditor:** Independent and qualified
- **IT Security Specialist:** Certified professional

### 3.3 Customer Protection
- **Deposit Insurance:** Coverage through Nigeria Deposit Insurance Corporation (NDIC)
- **Grievance Redressal:** Dedicated customer service unit
- **Refund Policy:** Clear procedures for failed/reversed transactions
- **Dispute Resolution:** CBN-approved dispute resolution mechanism

### 3.4 Fee Structure
All fees and charges must be:
- Transparent and disclosed to customers
- Approved by CBN
- Competitive and reasonable
- Displayed on all platforms

---

## 4. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

The Licensee is strictly prohibited from:
1. Engaging in any form of gambling transactions
2. Processing transactions for sanctioned entities
3. Operating without adequate capital reserves
4. Misrepresenting services or rates
5. Failing to report required information to CBN
6. Violating foreign exchange regulations
7. Engaging in fraudulent activities

---

## 5. CONDITIONS OF LICENSE

### 5.1 Renewal Requirements
- Submit renewal application 3 months before expiration
- Provide audited financial statements
- Demonstrate continued compliance with all regulations
- Pay renewal fees as prescribed by CBN

### 5.2 Inspection Rights
CBN reserves the right to:
- Conduct unannounced inspections
- Review all records and transactions
- Interview staff and management
- Access all systems and facilities

### 5.3 License Suspension
The CBN may suspend this license if the Licensee:
- Violates any provision of this permit
- Fails to maintain required capital
- Engages in fraudulent activities
- Poses risk to financial system stability
- Fails to comply with regulatory directives

### 5.4 License Revocation
The CBN may revoke this license if the Licensee:
- Commits serious violations
- Becomes insolvent
- Engages in criminal activities
- Fails to remedy suspension causes
