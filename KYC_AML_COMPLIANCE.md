## 1. POLICY STATEMENT

### 1.1 Commitment
Global Bank Nigeria Limited ("GBN" or "the Bank") is committed to maintaining the highest standards of Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance. We recognize our critical role in preventing financial crimes and protecting the integrity of the Nigerian financial system.

### 1.2 Objectives
- Prevent the Bank from being used for money laundering, terrorist financing, or other illegal activities
- Comply with all relevant Nigerian laws and international standards
- Protect the Bank's reputation and maintain public confidence
- Safeguard customers and the financial system from financial crimes
- Foster a culture of compliance throughout the organization

### 1.3 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All business units and departments of GBN
- All employees, contractors, and agents
- All customers and counterparties
- All products and services offered by GBN
- All transactions processed by GBN

## 3. KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC) REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Customer Identification
All customers must provide valid identification documents:

**Individual Customers:**
- Primary ID: International Passport, National ID Card, Driver's License, or Voter's Card
- Secondary ID: Any additional government-issued ID
- Proof of Address: Utility bill, tenancy agreement, or bank statement (not older than 3 months)
- Passport photograph: Recent, clear, and colored
- BVN (Bank Verification Number): Mandatory for all customers

**Corporate Customers:**
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Articles and Memorandum of Association
- Board Resolution authorizing account opening
- List of Directors and Shareholders (Ultimate Beneficial Owners)
- Tax Identification Certificate
- Utility bill of registered office
- Valid ID of authorized signatories
- Company seal

### 3.2 KYC Classification

**Tier 1 (Low Risk) - Basic Requirements:**
- Name, address, and telephone number
- Valid ID document
- BVN
- Passport photograph
- Transaction limits: ₦50,000 per transaction, ₦300,000 monthly

**Tier 2 (Medium Risk) - Enhanced Requirements:**
- All Tier 1 requirements
- Proof of address
- Source of funds/wealth
- Employment information
- Transaction limits: ₦1,000,000 per transaction, ₦10,000,000 monthly

**Tier 3 (High Risk) - Comprehensive Requirements:**
- All Tier 2 requirements
- Comprehensive source of funds documentation
- Financial statements (for businesses)
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring
- No transaction limits subject to CBN approval

### 3.3 High-Risk Categories
The following categories require enhanced due diligence:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Non-Resident Nigerians
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Casinos and gaming establishments
- Non-profit organizations
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex corporate structures
- Customers with adverse media

## 4. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 Money Laundering Risks
The Bank has identified the following money laundering risks:
- **Placement Risk:** Initial introduction of illicit funds
- **Layering Risk:** Complex transactions to obscure audit trail
- **Integration Risk:** Reintroduction of laundered funds into legitimate economy
- **Terrorist Financing Risk:** Funds supporting terrorist activities
- **Proliferation Financing Risk:** Financing of weapons of mass destruction

### 4.2 Risk-Based Approach
GBN implements a risk-based approach to AML:
- Conduct enterprise-wide risk assessment annually
- Allocate resources based on risk levels
- Implement enhanced controls for high-risk areas
- Regular review and update of risk assessments
- Document all risk management decisions

### 4.3 AML Controls
- **Transaction Screening:** Automated screening against sanctions lists
- **Pattern Recognition:** Detection of suspicious transaction patterns
- **Geographic Risk Monitoring:** Enhanced scrutiny of high-risk jurisdictions
- **Currency Reporting:** Threshold reporting for cash transactions
- **Wire Transfer Monitoring:** Enhanced scrutiny of international transfers
- **Correspondent Banking Due Diligence:** Regular review of partner banks

## 5. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)

### 5.1 Standard CDD Process
For all customers, GBN will:
1. Collect and verify customer identity
2. Understand the nature and purpose of customer relationship
3. Conduct ongoing monitoring of transactions
4. Maintain up-to-date customer information
5. Assess and categorize customer risk level

### 5.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
For high-risk customers, GBN will:
- Obtain additional identification documents
- Conduct more frequent reviews
- Obtain senior management approval
- Implement enhanced transaction monitoring
- Require source of wealth/funds documentation
- Conduct periodic site visits
- Obtain information on reason for transactions

### 5.3 Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)
For low-risk customers (subject to CBN approval), GBN may:
- Simplify verification requirements
- Reduce monitoring frequency
- Streamline documentation processes

### 5.4 Ongoing Monitoring
GBN will:
- Monitor customer transactions regularly
- Update customer information periodically
- Review customer risk assessment annually
- Conduct periodic transaction reviews
- Update KYC records when material changes occur

## 6. TRANSACTION MONITORING

### 6.1 Monitoring Systems
GBN employs automated transaction monitoring systems that:
- Screen all transactions against predefined rules
- Generate alerts for suspicious activities
- Conduct real-time and batch monitoring
- Support manual review and investigation
- Maintain comprehensive audit trails

### 6.2 Monitoring Rules
Transactions triggering review include:
- Cash transactions above ₦5,000,000
- International transfers above $10,000
- Multiple small transactions (structuring)
- Rapid movement of funds
- Transactions with high-risk jurisdictions
- Unusual transaction patterns
- Inconsistent transaction behavior

### 6.3 Alert Management
All monitoring alerts will be:
- Reviewed by trained AML analysts
- Investigated within specified timeframes
- Documented with supporting evidence
- Escalated to management when appropriate
- Reported to authorities if suspicious

### 6.4 Suspicious Transaction Indicators
Red flags include:
- Large cash deposits or withdrawals
- Transactions with no apparent economic purpose
- Frequent cross-border transfers
- Use of multiple accounts by same individual
- Transactions involving shell companies
- Refusal to provide requested information
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile

## 7. SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING

### 7.1 Reporting Obligations
GBN is obligated to report:
- Suspicious Transactions to NFIU
- Currency Transactions above threshold
- Terrorist Financing activities
- PEP transactions and relationships
- Sanctions violations

### 7.2 Suspicious Transaction Report (STR)
An STR must be filed when:
- There are reasonable grounds to suspect money laundering
- Transactions have no apparent economic or lawful purpose
- Complexity or unusual nature of transaction
- Inconsistency with customer's known business
- Source of funds cannot be reasonably explained

### 7.3 Reporting Timeline
- **Immediate Reporting:** For ongoing or imminent threats
- **Within 7 days:** For suspicious transactions identified
- **Within 24 hours:** For terrorist financing activities
- **Monthly:** For currency transactions above threshold

### 7.4 Reporting Process
1. Identify suspicious activity
2. Document observations and evidence
3. Conduct internal investigation
4. Prepare STR with all relevant details
5. Submit to NFIU through approved channels
6. Maintain records of report
7. Follow up on NFIU requests

### 7.5 Non-Disclosure
GBN shall not:
- Inform customer of STR filing
- Disclose reporting to unauthorized parties
- Allow customer to influence reporting decision
- Delay reporting for any reason

## 8. RECORD KEEPING

### 8.1 Records to be Maintained
GBN shall maintain records for:
- Customer identification and KYC documents
- Account opening and relationship documents
- Transaction records and supporting documentation
- AML/CFT policies and procedures
- Risk assessments and due diligence reports
- Training records and certifications
- STRs and other regulatory reports
- Correspondence with regulators

### 8.2 Retention Period
- **Customer Records:** Minimum 5 years after account closure
- **Transaction Records:** Minimum 5 years after transaction
- **STRs and Regulatory Reports:** Minimum 5 years
- **Training Records:** Minimum 3 years
- **Risk Assessments:** Current and previous 3 years

### 8.3 Record Storage
- Records stored in secure, tamper-proof systems
- Both physical and electronic records maintained
- Regular backups and disaster recovery
- Access restricted to authorized personnel
- Audit trail for all record access

### 8.4 Record Accessibility
- Records readily accessible to regulators
- Quick retrieval systems in place
- Indexing and search capabilities
- Cross-border data sharing per regulations

## 9. STAFF TRAINING AND AWARENESS

### 9.1 Training Requirements
All employees must complete:
- **Initial Training:** Within 30 days of employment
- **Annual Refresher:** Mandatory update training
- **Role-Specific Training:** Specialized training for relevant roles
- **Management Training:** Executive and board training
- **New Hire Training:** Comprehensive AML/KYC induction

### 9.2 Training Content
Training will cover:

### 9.3 Training Delivery

- Online e-learning modules
- Workshops and seminars
- External training programs
- Regular awareness campaigns
- Updates on new regulations

### 9.4 Training Records
GBN will maintain:
- Training attendance records
- Assessment and test results
- Training completion certificates

## 10. COMPLIANCE ENFORCEMENT

### 10.1 Compliance Culture
GBN fosters a compliance culture through:
- Tone from the top - Board and executive commitment
- Clear communication of expectations
- Regular awareness campaigns
- Recognition of compliant behavior
- Zero tolerance for violations

### 10.2 Compliance Monitoring
- Regular compliance reviews and audits
- Testing of AML/CFT controls
- Monitoring of regulatory developments
- Gap analysis and remediation
- Key Risk Indicator (KRI) tracking

### 10.3 Internal Controls
- Segregation of duties
- Dual approval requirements
- System controls and validations
- Management oversight and review
- Independent audit function

### 10.4 Disciplinary Actions
Non-compliance may result in:
- Written warnings
- Suspension of duties
- Mandatory retraining
- Demotion or transfer
- Termination of employment
- Legal action where appropriate

### 10.5 Whistleblowing
GBN maintains a confidential whistleblowing mechanism:
- Anonymous reporting channels
- Protection from retaliation
- Investigation of all reports
- Feedback to whistleblowers where appropriate
